What flood zone 3 means

Land within flood zone 3 has a high probability of flooding from rivers and the sea.

You need to carry out a flood risk assessment (FRA) as part of the planning application for this development.

Next steps for your planning application

Decide what you need for your planning application

Contact your local planning authority to check their planning requirements.

Get a Flood Risk Assessment (FRA). You will normally need to pay a flood risk specialist to carry it out.

For developments in flood zone 3 your FRA should include and assessment of all sources of flooding and take into account climate change allowances.

Find out how to do an assessment.

To find out about other factors that might affect the flood risk of this location, you should also check:

  • surface water, groundwater and reservoir flood risk
  • Warrington planning authority's strategic flood risk assessment (SFRA), which includes future flood risk
Other data you may need

You will also need to assess residual risk from flood defence breach in this location. If available, we will provide breach hazard mapping data to help you do this.

Get help

For help in deciding what flood risk information you need, email the Environment Agency team in Yorkshire at neyorkshire@environment-agency.gov.uk

Order flood risk data for rivers and the sea

The flood risk data usually includes:

  • detailed flood data
  • historic flood event data
  • modelled flood levels and extents
  • flood defence locations and attributes

We aim to email you the data within 20 working days or sooner.
