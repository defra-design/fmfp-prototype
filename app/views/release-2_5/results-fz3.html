This location is in flood zone 3

What flood zone 3 means

Areas within flood zone 3 have a high probability of flooding from rivers and the sea.

You need to carry out a flood risk assessment as part of your planning application for a development in flood zone 3.

More information about flood zones.

What the flood map shows

The flood zones in the map:

  • are based on present day risk and the map shows the zone the site is in now
  • do not include climate change information
  • ignore the effect any flood defences shown could have

The flood map is suitable for a local planning authority to use when checking a planning application to see if a development is:

  • in flood zone 1, 2 or 3
  • within 20 metres of a main river or a flood defence
  • a water storage area (also likely to be flood zone 3b in the SFRA)

Decide what you need for your planning application

You should:

  • Speak to Warrington local authority to find what their planning requirements are
  • get a flood risk assessment, this is usually done by flood risk consultant

Order flood risk data for rivers and the sea

You can make a request using this service. Flood risk data should normally be emailed to you within 20 working days.

Depending on the information that's available, the flood risk data will include:

  • detailed flood data
  • historic flood event data
  • modelled flood levels and extents
  • flood defence locations and attributes

Some developments are more complex than others and this data isn’t always enough to allow you to produce a flood risk assessment. You should check what other information you might need to complete a flood risk assessment.
