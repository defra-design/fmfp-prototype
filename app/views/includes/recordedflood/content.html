Past floods

What the recorded flood outlines dataset is

The recorded flood outlines are a geographical information system (GIS) data layer that show our verified records of areas that have flooded in the past from:

  • rivers
  • the sea
  • groundwater
  • surface water

The complete recorded flood outlines dataset includes data quality flags for outlines recorded after April 2020. This indicates the confidence we have in an outline.

Past floods information included here

The recorded flood events included in this document are for areas of land local to your site location that have been flooded by any of these sources:

  • ephemeral water
  • main rivers
  • ordinary watercourses
  • the sea

Definitions

Ephemeral rivers and streams are temporary rivers that are caused by rainfall or melted snow.

A main river is usually a large river or stream. The Environment Agency designate and maintain them to manage flood risk.

An ordinary watercourse is a watercourse that is not part of a main river. This includes rivers, streams, ditches, cuts, culverts and dikes.

Data limitations

We may not have records of flooding in all areas. This does not mean that the area has not flooded.

Records began in 1946. We have limited details about flooding incidents before this.

The outlines show flooding to land, not individual properties. This means that some properties in the area might not have been affected by flooding.

It is also possible that the pattern of flooding in this area has changed. This may mean that the area could flood under different circumstances, or it might be less likely to.

The outlines do not include flooding from:

  • drainage where rainfall has led to surface water ponding or overland runoff
  • artificial, water-bearing sewer, water supply and wastewater treatment pipelines

Get flood information from other organisations

Contact Somerset County Council Lead local authority (LLFA) and Somerset Drainage board to get information about past flooding caused by surface water or drainage systems.

Changes to flood defences

The outlines factor in any flood defences, structures and other infrastructure that were in place when it flooded. But the flood defences may have been overtopped, breached or were blocked.

The defences (also known as assets) that were in place may also have changed. For example, assets may have been built more recently than the last recorded flood outline.
