Flood zones and what they mean

Flood zones are based on a locationâ€™s chance of flooding from rivers or the sea in any year. They are used in the planning process to decide how likely it is a development could flood.

Flood zones do not take into account other sources of flooding. This means a site in flood zone 1 could still flood.

Flood zone 1

Locations in flood zone 1 have a low probability of flooding. This means in any year they have a 0.1% chance of flooding in any year.

Some flood zone 1 developments need a flood risk assessment as part of their planning application. Find out when you need a flood risk assessment for development in flood zone 1.

Flood zone 2

Locations in flood zone 2 have a medium probability of flooding. This means in any year they have between a 1.0% and 0.5% chance of flooding from rivers or the sea.

Flood zone 2 developments need a flood risk assessment as part of their planning application.

Flood zone 3

Locations in flood zone 3 have a high probability of flooding. This means there is a 1% of more chance of flooding from rivers or the sea.

Flood zone 3 developments need to submit a flood risk assessment as part of their planning application. This determines if the site is flood zone 3a or 3b.

Find out how flood zone 3a and 3b are defined.

Flood defences

There are flood defences in part or all of the location you selected.

Flood defences:

  • reduce the probability of flooding from a specific source (a river or the sea), but do not completely remove the risk
  • can fail or a flood that is bigger than the one the defence is designed to protect against could happen
  • may provide reduced protection over time because of climate change increasing flood risk in the future

There are different types of flood defence. They include:

  • culverts
  • embankments
  • flood walls

You can view a complete list of flood defences, called asset categories, on environment.data.gov.uk.
